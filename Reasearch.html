Glacier Research

Ice Cores

Ice cores help us discover things about our past. Ice Cores are basically samples of glaciers. They cut out a cylinder downwards into the ice. There are many things you can get from an ice core sample. When ice forms, it contains little bubbles of air from the time it formed. So for ice buried deep into a glacier, their air bubbles can tell you about the climate back then. There are also layers in the ice core from which you can tell the history of advance and retreat, the darker layers are from sediment buildup on the surface from when the ice was exposed.

Ice Ages

There have been many ice ages in the past. The 5 main glacial ice ages, listed in order from oldest to youngest ; Huronian, cryogenical, Anedean-Saharan, Karoo, Quaternary. During the last ice age, one ice sheet called the Laurentide ice sheet, covered most of North America. The glacier extended as far as Louisiana. Scientists call ice ages Glacial Maximums. There have also been no ice caps for the last billion years, leading the sea level to be 70 meters higher. The main causes of ice ages have been labeled the Milankovitch Cycle. That is about how earth’s orbit, tilt, and wobbles on its axis are the main cause of the warming and cooling on earth. And from the milankovitch cycle, every 100,000 years the conditions are just enough to make sure the summer can’t melt away the previous winter’s ice.
